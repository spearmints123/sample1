Faces Name Description
Maria Clara
  • Maria Clara was a sweet and caring young lady. Maria Clara, who had been raised with affection, was well-loved by everyone around her and could be moved to acts of charity, as in the case of the leper. Maria Clara is referred to as Amelia in an early draft of Noli Me Tangere.
    		•
    Padre Damaso
  • Father Dámaso is a pedantic and haughty priest who is a Spanish friar living in the Philippines. Despite having spent more than twenty years hearing confessions from Filipinos, Father Dámaso hardly understands or speaks Tagalog, the language of the country.
    		•
    Kapitan Tiago
  • Captain Tiago, a man of Filipino descent and one of the region's wealthiest landowners. He is a powerful planter in San Diego, an affluent city where Tiago spends two months of the year. He is a devout Christian in addition to being wealthy and well-liked in government circles.
    		•
    Donya Victorina
  • Donya Victorina was a socialite and Capitan Tiago's friend. She and her husband, Don Tiburcio, sought to associate with San Diego's influential figures in order to advance their social standing.
    		•
    Crisostimo Ibarra
  • Crisostomo Ibarra was born into the Ibarra family and was raised by his father, Don Rafael. He became friends with Maria Clara as a child in San Diego. He was an honest, and creative idealist.
